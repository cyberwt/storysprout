# StorySprout — 2 minute 20 second demo video script

Record this as a public YouTube video. Keep it under three minutes, show the app working, and include the Codex/GPT-5.6 explanation required by the challenge.

## Before recording

- Start the app with `npm run dev`.
- Use the default demo names: Maya and Poppy.
- Set the visual world to **Cozy watercolor**.
- Choose, in order: **A brave little lantern**, **Sing a silly song**, and **Share brave stories**.
- Turn on system audio only if the browser voice sounds good. Your own voice-over is more important than app audio.
- Record in a browser window large enough to show the interface clearly.

## Script

### 0:00–0:15 — The idea

> This is StorySprout, a warm, choice-led bedtime storybook for children. It is inspired by the way children naturally tell stories while playing with toys. Instead of simply watching a tale, they make the choices that shape it.

Show the welcome page, then click **Start the demo story**.

### 0:15–0:35 — Set up the story

> For this polished demo, I created Poppy and the Lost Star. The child adds their name and their companion’s name, then chooses the world the story will live in.

Show “Maya,” “Poppy,” and select Cozy watercolor. Click **Begin Poppy’s story**.

### 0:35–1:20 — Show the co-creation loop

> Poppy sees a star fall into the Whispering Woods. At each scene, the child makes one small but meaningful decision. Here, I’ll pack a brave little lantern.

Show scene one and click the lantern choice.

> Next, Poppy meets a shy firefly. I’ll choose to sing a silly song, helping the firefly feel brave.

Show scene two and select the song.

> Finally, Poppy finds the lost star. I’ll promise to share brave stories.

Show scene three and select the promise.

### 1:20–1:45 — The payoff

> The completed book now reflects the choices I made. It can be read aloud in the browser, and it ends with an off-screen play idea so the story can continue beyond the device.

Show the completed book and briefly click **Listen to the whole story** if the narration is ready. Point to the real-world play prompt.

### 1:45–2:15 — Codex and GPT-5.6 explanation

> I used Codex with GPT-5.6 to move from product idea to working demo: shaping the child-centered flow, implementing the React experience, refining the original visual system, turning each choice into a natural story ending, and verifying the production build. Codex helped me iterate quickly while I focused on the emotional boundaries of the product: StorySprout is a creative co-play tool, never a replacement for a caregiver.

**Important:** Say this only if it accurately describes the model and Codex session you used. If needed, replace “GPT-5.6” with the exact model name shown in your Codex session—but the challenge requires GPT-5.6, so verify this before recording.

### 2:15–2:20 — Close

> StorySprout helps every little idea become a world. Thank you.
